The Dance of Appreciation and Inflation


In the ever-evolving landscape of real estate investments, understanding the dynamics of appreciation and inflation is paramount. While inflation may erode the purchasing power of the dollar, real estate emerges as a resilient hedge. Annual home prices often outpace published inflation rates, making it a compelling investment.

Consider the Rule of 72, a timeless formula predicting how long it takes for an investment to double in value. Imagine a $400,000 home appreciating at a consistent 10% annually. According to the rule, it would take just 7.2 years for that home to double in value, reaching a staggering $800,000. Simultaneously, inflation makes mortgage debt more affordable over time, contributing to the allure of real estate investments.

Supply and Demand: Decoding the Real Estate Market


Navigating the real estate market is akin to mastering a dance between supply and demand. One invaluable metric to gauge the market's pulse is the Days on Market (DOM). As of February 2022, DOM numbers reached an all-time low of just 18 days, signaling a robust seller's market.

For instance, a buyer eyeing properties in Issaquah could scrutinize DOM trends to identify market equilibrium. Extended DOM numbers may hint at overpriced listings, presenting an opportunity for strategic negotiations. Our local market in King County consistently outperforms national trends, fostering higher appreciation rates.

Demystifying Inflation: The True Numbers


Inflation, a seemingly elusive concept, holds profound implications for real estate. Official government-published inflation data may not tell the whole story, with speculations that the actual numbers surpass official figures. Governments may underreport inflation to manage entitlement programs tied to published rates.

Digging deeper, modifications to inflation measurement methods since 1980 raise questions about accuracy. If measured today using earlier standards, inflation rates might soar to 15%. Vegetable prices, for instance, surged by 80.6% year-over-year by November 2022, painting a vivid picture of the true economic landscape.
